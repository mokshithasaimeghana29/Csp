Project Abstract

This community service project examines the preferences of individuals for government versus private hospitals, aiming to identify factors that shape healthcare decisions. Through surveys, interviews, and data analysis, we explore how communities choose their healthcare providers.

Key Objectives and Insights

  • Purpose: Understand why people prefer government or private hospitals to inform healthcare policy improvements.
  • Methodology: Utilize surveys and interviews to gather diverse perspectives from urban and rural communities.
  • Affordability: Highlight cost as a major driver for choosing government hospitals, especially among low-income groups.
  • Quality Perception: Identify private hospitals’ appeal due to advanced facilities and specialized care.
  • Accessibility: Analyze how geographic location impacts hospital choice, with government hospitals serving rural areas better.
  • Trust Dynamics: Explore varying levels of trust in public versus private healthcare systems.
  • Community Impact: Provide data to support equitable healthcare reforms that balance cost and quality.
  • Engagement: Foster community involvement to ensure findings reflect real-world needs and experiences.

The project underscores the importance of community-driven research in addressing healthcare disparities. By highlighting these insights, we aim to contribute to policies that enhance healthcare access and quality for all.
