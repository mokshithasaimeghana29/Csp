IDENTIFICATION OF PEOPLE’S PREFERENCES FOR GOVERNMENT VS PRIVATE HOSPITALS

Project Key Insights

  • Cost Sensitivity: Many prefer government hospitals due to lower costs, especially for low-income communities.
  • Quality Perception: Private hospitals are often favored for perceived higher quality of care and advanced facilities.
  • Accessibility: Government hospitals are more accessible in rural areas, while private hospitals dominate urban settings.
  • Trust Factors: Trust in government hospitals varies, with private hospitals gaining favor for specialized treatments.
  • Community Impact: Preferences highlight the need for policy reforms to balance affordability and quality in healthcare.

Welcome to our community service project dedicated to understanding healthcare choices. This initiative explores why individuals prefer government or private hospitals, aiming to inform better healthcare policies and services through public insights.

Through surveys, data analysis, and community engagement, we seek to highlight the factors—such as cost, quality of care, accessibility, and trust—that shape these preferences. Join us in uncovering how healthcare systems can better serve our communities.
